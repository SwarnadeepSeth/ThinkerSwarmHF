Microsoft (MSFT) Q3 FY2025 Earnings Report - April 30, 2025
=====================================================

QUARTERLY RESULTS (Q3 FY2025 - Ended March 31, 2025)
--------------------------------------------
- Revenue: $70.1 billion (up 13% YoY, 15% in constant currency)
- EPS: $3.46 (up 18% YoY, 19% in constant currency)
- Net Income: $25.8 billion (up 18% from $21.9 billion)
- Operating Income: Up 16% YoY (19% in constant currency)

SEGMENT PERFORMANCE
------------------
1. Intelligent Cloud (Azure):
   - Azure revenue grew 33% (16 points from AI)
   - Expected Q4 Azure growth: 34-35% in constant currency
   - FY25 capex: $80 billion on AI data centers
   - AI capacity constraints expected beyond June 2025

2. Productivity and Business Processes:
   - Revenue: $32.05-$32.35 billion expected (11-12% growth)
   - Microsoft 365 Commercial: 12% growth, 7% seat growth
   - Microsoft 365 Consumer subscribers: 87.7 million (+9%)
   - LinkedIn revenue: +7%
   - Dynamics 365 revenue: +16%

3. More Personal Computing:
   - Revenue expected: $12.35-$12.85 billion
   - Windows OEM: Expected to decline mid-high single digits
   - Search advertising: Strong growth expected

GUIDANCE (Q4 FY2025)
-------------------
- Total Revenue: $73.15-$74.25 billion
- Intelligent Cloud: $28.75-$29.05 billion (20-22% growth)
- Azure AI services: Demand growing faster than capacity
- COGS: $23.6-$23.8 billion (19-20% growth)
- Operating expense: $18-$18.1 billion (~5% growth)
- Operating margins: Expected up slightly YoY

KEY CATALYSTS
------------
1. AI Leadership: Azure 33% growth with 16 points from AI services
2. Strong commercial cloud momentum across segments
3. $80B capex investment in AI infrastructure
4. 87.7M Microsoft 365 subscribers (9% growth)
5. Strong FY25 guidance signals confidence

RISKS
-----
1. Tariff concerns: $80B AI infrastructure requires overseas imports
2. Windows OEM declining due to inventory normalization
3. AI capacity constraints limiting near-term growth
4. Foreign currency headwinds (~3% impact)

MANAGEMENT SENTIMENT
-------------------
CEO Satya Nadella: "Demand is growing faster than we can bring capacity online"
Strong Q4 guidance shows confidence in AI-driven growth
Focus on scaling AI infrastructure while maintaining margins
